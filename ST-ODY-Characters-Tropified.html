Character Sheet: Star Trek: Odyssey

The unofficial TV Tropes wiki edition

This document outlines the main and recurring characters from the unofficial Star Trek: Odyssey series, based on the Star Trek Online MMO and prior canon prime-universe Star Trek shows from The Next Generation onwards.

Its main purpose is to serve as a Writer's Guide to defining their personalities, backstories, relationships/interactions between and potential storylines revolving around them - tropes, characterizations and other narrative devices are styled in bold for clarity, and definitions can be found on the official Television Tropes & Idioms website.

Understandably, a large number of characters are Tuckerizations of their real-life inspirations, but given the setting of the series and the vastly different backstories, we shouldn't get into any trouble or complaints about "demeaning" fictional portrayals.

Main Cast (in order of billing)

Captain Robin L. Zhang

Voiced by: TBD

The Commanding Officer (CO) of the titular Federation starship USS Odyssey, a relatively young Oriental male human in his late twenties to early thirties with years of experience as a Starfleet officer, yet new to his role as The Captain.

Whilst neither the dashing adventurer like Kirk, nor the astute diplomat like Picard, Zhang's greatest assets are his idealism, strong sense of morals and his ability to capitalize on the individual talents of his crew, although occasionally plagued by moments of doubt.

  • Authority Equals Asskicking: Regularly leads away team missions, and can hold his own against some of the larger threats.
    • Justified due to his past experience as a Security Officer aboard the Lakota; it is mentioned in dialogue that he fought in the Second Battle of Ajilon Prime during the early years of Federation-Klingon war, among other engagements.
  • The Anti-Nihilist: His motivation for serving in Starfleet.
  • Berserk Button: Woe befall anyone who threatens his ship, his crew, whoever he is protecting, his trusted allies or the Federation as a whole. Throw in a bit of Determinator for good measure, and there will be hell to pay.
  • Bold Explorer: Deep down inside, although somewhat subdued given the current reality that the Federation can't spare nearly as much resources for peaceful exploration as they can for defending their territory.
  • The Captain: Part of the series is about him learning the ropes, as this is his first command.
  • The Chains of Commanding: Gradually feels the weight of these over time, as he is forced to make more and more decisions would elicit from his friends-turned-subordinates anything from concern to outright protests. Made all the worse by the fact that Starfleet Captains are supposed to appear invincible for the sake of crew morale.
  • Chronic Hero Syndrome: The smallest injustice can really rub him the wrong way.
  • Designated Victim: How do you show that the bad guys mean business? Punch Zhang!
    • No thanks to his Chronic Hero Syndrome, of course.
    • Downplayed in that most of the cast are more-or-less equally susceptible to being Victim of the Week; it's just that shit happens to Zhang a little more often than to other characters.
  • Drink Order: Exceptionally fond of loose-leaf teas. Zhang: Herbal/Peppermint/Green Tea, Hot.
  • Fantastic Racism: Has a deep-seated and irrational hatred of Pakleds, a race universally known for their slovenly attitudes and mooching off passing ships.
    • Revealed to have stemmed from bad childhood experiences in "Death In Paradise" - Zhang's father worked at a Starfleet shipyard, and had a Pakled colleague who regularly took credit for the former's work; consequently, Zhang's father had to work overtime to make up for it, and so often missed out on his son's birthdays.
    • As much as Zhang doesn't want to admit it, it was ironically the testimony of another Pakled at a Court-Martial that saved the former from murder charges in said episode.
  • A Father to His Men: Cares very deeply about the physical and emotional well-being of his crew, as well as friends serving aboard other Starfleet ships.
    • Stemmed from Must Make Amends for his more callous, selfish and attention-seeking days from the Academy.
  • Man Child: When off-duty, to Barber's annoyance.
  • Military Brat: Backstory peppered throughout the series suggests that generations of his family have served in Starfleet, as far back as a nameless crewman aboard the NX-01 Enterprise.
  • The Movie Buff: One of his personal Holodeck programs is a recreation of a turn-of-the-millennium cinema, which he sometimes retreats when faced with a dilemma, or at the end of an episode where he just happens to be watching a film depicting a scenario similar to the Problem of the Week the crew has just faced to deliver An Aesop.
  • My Greatest Failure: Two.
    • His attempts at Dogged Nice Guy and subsequent Not Good with Rejection destroyed his early friendship with Barber at the Academy, and estranged him briefly from their mutual classmates, all off-screen before the series. It took a major Relationship-Salvaging Disaster involving one doomed ship for them to even start reconciling.
    • Unquestionably obeying orders from Admiral Zelle to eliminate a group of Romulan scientists, who were suspected of developing banned subspace weapons. Turns out they were civilian medical researchers develop methods to detect Undine/Species 8472 infiltrators... like Zelle herself. Basically, being an Unwitting Pawn.
  • Screw The Rules, I'm Doing What's Right!: Disobeyed Admiral T'nae's direct orders and official policy against interfering in the affairs of non-aligned states, to assist the Reman uprising against their Romulan Star Empire remnant oppressors.
  • The Fettered
  • What the Hell, Hero?: Usually delivered by Barber, but most of the senior staff have done so at least once.
  • You Are In Command Now: Flashbacks from Season Two episode "Currahee" reveal that some time after escaping the destruction of the Bellerophon, Zhang served as Tactical Officer aboard the USS Currahee, which was involved in the disasterous First Contact incident with the nomadic Endari. With the original Captain incapacitated and the First Officer killed, Zhang assumed command and was able to guide the ship and its surviving crew to safety; consequently, Starfleet Command would award him command of the Odyssey.

Commander Thomas Richardson

Voiced by: TBD

The Odyssey's level-headed Caucasian male human First Officer, and Zhang's best friend from the Academy. Despite a well-placed father, he dodged every attempt at nepotism and insisted on climbing through the ranks through his own merits.

Confident, steadfast and reliable, he is the voice of reason amongst the senior staff, serving as Zhang's confidant, conscience and sounding board.

  • Action Hero: More so than Zhang. Heck, the guy even has zero-G EVA combat training.
  • Beta Couple: Richardson/Tash is this to Zhang/Barber.
  • Commanding Coolness: Whenever he's left in charge of the Odyssey whilst Zhang is away.
  • The Creon: Despite being alluded as a better choice for commanding the Odyssey (or any other ship, for that matter), he chooses to remain as First Officer out of loyalty.
  • The Confidant
  • Expy: Started out as a toned-down version of Commander Riker from The Next Generation.
  • First Name Basis: Zhang prefers to call him "Tom" rather than the customary "Number One" used for First Officers, even in an official setting, much to the consternation of other Starfleet captains.
  • Happily Married: To Tash, from Season Two onwards.
  • The Heart: Has to constantly remind everyone else what is really at stake whenever somebody's personal vendetta / angst gets in the way of things.
  • The Kirk: In his advisory role to Zhang, he has to balance out Barber's straightforward and somewhat impulsive tactical attitudes, with Remara's more calculated and reserved pacifism.
  • The Lancer: To Zhang's introspective brooding tendencies.
  • Lovable Jock: Backstory reveals he was captain of the Academy's Velocity team (handball with Phaser pistols and Frisbees), but gets on quite well with the geekier members of the cast.
  • Military Brat: Admiral Richardson is the head of Starfleet's Paymaster Corps.
  • Nice Guy
  • Nice to the Waiter: Goes out of his way to thank the waiter after a meal at the small Bajoran eatery during "Of Bajor".
    • Might have had something to do with seeing how the staff were treated by Klingon patrons beforehand.
  • Number Two
  • Perma Stubble
  • Sensitive Guy and Manly Man: Zhang as the former and Richardson as the latter.
  • Take a Third Option: Often reminds Zhang whenever a dilemma is actually a False Dichotomy.
  • Trademark Favourite Food: Pies. There's a reason "Pieday Fridays" are named as such, you know.
  • True Companions: He and Ong are this to Zhang.
  • The Watson: Despite carrying an air of confidence, he isn't afraid to invite Exposition from Remara, Barber or whichever senior office the Problem of the Week is most applicable to, in order to clarify the finer details of the situation at hand.

Lieutenant Commander Christine Barber

Voiced by: Caitlin Glass

The Tactical Officer aboard the Odyssey and Zhang's Love Interest, a no-nonsense, highly assertive and competent brunette who'd much rather shoot first and ask questions later. Also holds additional duties as Second and Helm Officer, due to the Federation's more militaristic stance in the series.

  • Ace Pilot / The Navigator: Hence her additional duties as the Odyssey's main helm officer. One episode provides the backstory that she once served as a test pilot for Starfleet's experimental Yellowstone-class Runabouts.
  • Action Girl: Although her role officially limits her to operating the Odyssey's shields and weapons, she beams down to away missions involving ground combat almost as often as Shran (who as Security Chief is supposed to fill said role).
    • In "Bringing Down the House", she substitutes for an injured Klingon in a Bat'leth tournament, and actually wins the finals by defeating seasoned opponents much stronger than her.
  • Aloof Dark-Haired Girl: Downplayed. Aloof yes, but technically, it's a shade of chestnut brown.
  • Berserk Button:
    • Anything remotely bad that happens to Tigan will set off Barber's Big Sister Instincts.
    • Anything that she thinks demeans women in general, real and perceived, even if unintentional.
    • Civilian freighters in danger. A Troubled Backstory Flashback in "Exitus Acta Probat" reveals that when she was a child, a Klingon pirate attacked her parents' freighter, and seriously injured her family.
  • Brutal Honesty: Ouch. Barber: I don't sugar-coat things.
  • Combat Pragmatist: Once encountered a Klingon warrior who threw aside his disruptor rifle and taunted her with an impressive display of swordsmanship with his Mek'leth. She simply threw a photon grenade.
  • Cool Alien Pet: Mr. Snuggles the Tribble.
  • Cool Big Sis: To Shran and Tigan, despite being only marginally older and unrelated race-wise. Both admire her for her professionalism and tough-as-nails attitude (not that they actually learn anything from her). On the flipside, this makes Barber somewhat more protective of them.
    • Also Helna, to a lesser extent.
  • Deadpan Snarker: Apparently so ingrained that one episode pokes fun at this - Barber is unexpectedly missing for most of one episode, and awkward pauses in dialogue between other characters highlight points where she would have cut in.
  • Defrosting Ice Queen AND Relationship Upgrade: Happened off-screen with Zhang in the pilot episode, sometime before the present day. Judging by their interactions, however, it is still very much a work in progress.
  • Disapproving Look: When she can't find the words to call out on others.
  • Embarrassing Nickname: "Squirt" - an Affectionate Nickname given by her brother Stephen due to the significant age difference between them (When Christine was five, Stephen was already in his mid-teens and helping out on their parents' freighter).
  • Honest Advisor / No Badass to His Valet: Not afraid to tell Zhang exactly what she thinks of him / his decisions, in situations that would reduce even the level-headed Richardson to borderline Yes-man.
  • Letting Her Hair Down: Usually worn up in a professional French twist / high bun, Barber loosens it down to mid-back length once off-duty. Her personality also softens noticeably when this occurs.
  • Noodle Incident: It's never elaborated just what exactly Zhang did that resulted in his early estrangement from Barber.
  • Token Wholesome: While Shran and Tigan fool around in skimpy swimsuits in the obligatory Beach Episode "Death in Paradise", Barber is shown lounging on a deck chair with a more modest sleeveless top, knee-length shorts and sunhat.
  • Tsundere (Type 1): Normally straightforward and professional to the rest of the crew, and very rarely shows her softer side.
    • Being Personal Isn't Professional is possibly her justification.
  • Obstructive Love Interest: Downplayed. While she doesn't berate Zhang openly, she sometimes still interprets his actions and behaviour in the worst possible way.
  • Red Oni, Blue Oni: In many situations she prefers a direct (and often tactical) strategy, in contrast to Science Officer Remara's ostensibly more pacifistic and diplomatic (as well as occasionally underhanded) approach. This causes them to butt heads almost every other episode when engaged in Teeth-Clenched Teamwork.
    • Lampshaded in that the departmental colours on their uniforms are Red and Blue, respectively.
  • Running Gag: Whenever Barber needs an excuse to get out of an unwanted social gathering, to look busy or justify why she's often already in the Ready Room with Zhang when someone else comes in, she always mentions that she "needs to do / is working on / was just handing in her Tactical Report". Thing is, nobody really knows what a Tactical Report is.
  • Space People: Was born on and spent most of her childhood aboard the civilian merchant ship her parents owned.
  • When She Smiles: How the rest of the senior staff, especially Zhang, feel about her.

Lieutenant Commander Geoff Ong

Voiced by: TBD

The Odyssey's Operations Officer, a relaxed, sociable Oriental male with the side-swept mop-top responsible for shipboard power/personnel/resource allocation, as well as handling subspace communications. Well-liked and trusted by just about the entire main cast, he is essentially, as one character describes it, "the structural integrity forcefield that keeps this family from flying apart at the seams".

  • Bash Brothers: With Richardson. If they happen to be beamed down in the same Away Team, chances are there'll be a scene of them backing each other up in a fight at least once in said episode.
  • Best Friend Manual: Knows the intricacies of the entire senior staff's personalities and how to help them mesh together properly.
  • Communications Officer
  • A Day in the Limelight: The subplot from one Season Three episode depicts a typical day of his job as Operations Officer, and while the issues he deals with are relatively mundane compared to the adventures the entire cast collectively go on over the course of the series, it's clear that without him chaos would reign ship-wide.
  • Extracurricular Enthusiast: Amongst the numerous clubs he was involved in back in his cadet days were the Academy Band and Glee Club (yes, it's a legitimate name with historical precedent and not just a TV show). Such interests carry over readily to the Odyssey, as he performs regularly on Jazz Nights in the Officer's Mess.
  • Fan of the Past: Besides being extremely well-versed in late-20th / early-21st Century popular culture references, turns of phrase and cultural conventions, he owns and dotes over a genuine Fender Stratocaster. He even offers advice for the type of seating and food to be used in Zhang's personal cinema Holodeck program.
  • For Happiness: Always has a trick or two to cheer everyone up. The crew even unofficially dubbed him as their "Chief Morale Officer".
  • Nice Guy
  • The Power of Friendship: Very good at invoking this, especially with Rubber-Forehead Aliens of the Week. Of course, he's perceptive enough to know whether someone is a lost cause or untrustworthy in the first place.
  • Revolving Door Band: Leads a Jazz Band lineup whose other members are composed of various random enlisted crew members who we seldom see or hear from again.
    • Results in the Running Gag of I am The Band, when crew members in the audience would gush over Ong's musical talents, but have no idea who the other musicians are.
    • Subverted from Season Three onwards, when K'vov joins in with the flabbjellah (traditional Andorian weapon/instrument combo) and Remara starts dabbling on the piano.
  • Ship Tease: With Tigan, given that they're both straightforward fun-loving people.
    • And very briefly, with Lieutenant Mitsune "Kitsune" Konno, his professional counterpart from the USS Hinata.
  • The Social Expert: An extension of Best Friend Manual to non-colleagues.
  • True Companions: He and Richardson are this to Zhang.

Lieutenant Commander Mirra Shran

Voiced by: TBD

The Odyssey's Security Chief, a passionate and alluring female Andorian who was Tactical Officer Barber's Academy roommate. In addition to coordinating shipboard security, accompanying dignitaries and escorting Away Teams, Shran is usually stationed on the bridge providing detailed tactical and weapons readiness information.

  • Action Girl
  • Berserk Button:
    • Touching her head antennae without consent.
    • Making her bleed in a fight unlocks her Determinator tendencies.
  • Bridge Bunny: Downplayed. When not on an Away Mission, her role is effectively to call out tactical sensor data ("Multiple Klingon warbirds, bearing two six five mark one nine!") or weapons/shields status ("Aft shields at seventy-three percent!").
  • Green-Skinned Space Babe: Except that Andorians are blue with white hair.
  • Famous Ancestor / Continuity Nod: Andorian Imperial Guard Commander Thy'lek Shran from Star Trek: Enterprise.
  • Fantastic Slur: Subverted in that her use of the Andorian slur for humans ("pinkskins") is a form of endearment towards Barber, her Cool Big Sis - who actually likes it.
    • The prologue of pilot episode "Azura" has then-Ensign Shran also refer to then-Ensign Zhang as a "cute pinkskin", while the latter is having a spat with then-Ensign Barber.
    • Then-Ensign Zhang's response in the heat of the moment? "Can it, smurfette!"
  • Far-Out Foreigner's Favourite Food: Inverted. Chocolate is poisonous to Andorians.
  • Fire-Forged Friends: In the prologue, then-Ensign Zhang thought of Ensign Shran as Barber's silly Andorian friend, and is visibly annoyed when she tries to play matchmaker while he and Barber were having a spat. He has to put these feelings aside when Barber is trapped under fallen debris and they have to work together to get her out before the Bellerophon blows up with them still on board. It is later indicated that in the off-screen events between the prologue and the maiden voyage of the Odyssey, Shran and Zhang got on much better terms with each other, even being assigned to the same Security Team together while serving aboard another Starfleet ship - this eventually leads Zhang to pick her as his Security Chief.
  • Hard Drinking Party Girl: When off-duty.
  • Hidden Depths: Underneath Shran's wild, outgoing and flirtatious veneer turns out to be a significant amount of insecurity and angst, as she consistently struggles to fit in with fellow Andorians due to their culture favouring reservedness and strict obedience. The discovery of this facet comes as a complete shock to Barber, who has supposedly been very close friends with Shran for many years.
    • After "Mind Games" established that she was very mildly telepathic due to having an Aenar as an distant ancestor, "Whispers in the Dark" revealed that as a child, she hated and feared being alone because the silence would heighten her sensitivity to the passionate thoughts and emotions of other Andorians, and so she developed an extroverted personality preferring the company of others in order to drown out these stray "voices".
  • Manic Pixie Dream Girl: Likes to think of herself as this to K'vov.
  • Ms. Fanservice: The sultry type, especially in the obligatory Beach Episode "Death in Paradise".
  • Mythology Gag: Like her ancestors in "The Aenar" from Star Trek: Enterprise, she mentions having struggled in the theoretical science courses at the Academy.
  • Rubber-Forehead Alien: Those two knobbed head antennae all Andorians have.
  • Security Officer
  • Shipper on Deck: Especially likes playing matchmaker for junior officers.
  • Ship Tease: In one of the later season episodes, Shran appears to be impressed by Remara's polite suaveness; by the end of said episode, however, they decide they were Better As Friends and she goes back to pining after K'vov.
  • Show Some Leg: Shran isn't above invoking this to help accomplish vital objectives.
    • In "Treasure Trading Station", she poses as a dabo girl (Ferengi roulette wheel operator) on Drozana Station to distract the uncooperative bar proprietor, whilst some junior security officers from the Odyssey override the security lockout to the station's seedier lower levels.
    • Repeated in "The Lost City of Paradise", where she masquerades as a prostitute and sweet-talks a small-time criminal into divulging details about a secret Thalaron weapons deal.
  • Tomboyish Ponytail: Always swept over her right shoulder to the front.
    • Playing with the very tip of it constitutes her Thinking Tic.

Lieutenant Commander Corat Remara

Voiced by: Vic Mignogna

The Odyssey's Chief Science Officer, a mild-mannered and polite Cardassian male whose main role is to basically explain the Technobabble principles behind exotic alien tech and highlight the dangers of the Negative Space Wedgie of the Week. His reserved but friendly demeanour and Cheshire Cat Grin hides a troubled past and loneliness.

Remara's assignment to the Odyssey was an interesting one; ostensibly he was there to monitor the use of an improved Deflector system he designed for the ship's experimental Quantum Slipstream Drive, but in reality the rest Admiralty was annoyed that the newly-commissioned Zhang had pulled connections to hand-pick most of his crew, and sought to take him down a notch. Despite initial apprehension, the other officers would gradually warm to Remara (in varying degrees) over the course of the series.

  • Bunny-Ears Lawyer: For all his idiosyncrasies, Remara demonstrates an unparalleled brilliance and adaptability at his job, especially when dealing with Imported Alien Phlebotinum. Not to mention that he's the sole designer (and by extension the only one capable of maintaining) the Black Box that allows the Odyssey's Faster-Than-Light Travel engine to go Up to Eleven, so they kind of need him around.
  • Bold Explorer: The Classic type. Often makes it known to others that Starfleet should also remember its roots as this.
  • Character Filibuster: Especially when sprouting Exposition - he's also very proud of his Technobabble.
  • Crazy Cultural Comparison: For most of the series, Barber is continually repulsed at Remara's overtly gentlemanly front that she believes is insincere mockery, whilst he in turn is clearly uncomfortable with her consistently snide remarks. She later finds out late in Season Three (to her horror) that traditional Cardassian courtship involves couples bitterly snapping at each other, and that Remara was actually very diplomatically trying to rebuff what he perceived as inappropriate advances.
    • Played for laughs as most of the crew already knew this all along, but just wanted to see her reaction.
  • Dark and Troubled Past: After the falling out with his family over his choice of profession, he struck out on his own, barely managing to find a small seedy apartment to live in while studying at the University of Culat on Cardassia Prime. His Science Ministry assignments after graduation were almost always solitary due to his inability to relate well with his (predominantly female) colleagues. To top it off, after being invited by his older brother (who always cared for him deeply and often visited him) to a major family reunion, he arrived at the venue just in time to watch his entire immediate and extended family, along with many other civilians, get wiped out in a in a True Way terrorist bombing attack. Having nowhere else to go served as the impetus to him pursuing a career in Starfleet.
  • Eating Lunch Alone: For the first two seasons he is often seen sitting by himself in the Officer's Mess, punctuated by rare dialogues of major insight with Zhang or off-duty verbal sparring with Barber.
  • Good with Numbers: Even while under fire in Season Five episode "Spheres of Influence", he manages to compute in his head the complex settings needed to keep the Iconian Gateway stable long enough for everyone to evacuate through.
  • Insufferable Genius: Most people's first - and Barber's persistent - impression of him.
    • Toned down after Season Three episode "The Ties That Bind", when a supposed reunion with his older brother missing since the bomb blast turned out to be a case of Dead Person Impersonation he failed to see through.
  • Intelligence Equals Isolation: Played straight for the first three seasons, somewhat toned down afterwards.
  • Last Name Basis: Practically everyone addresses him as "Remara", with rank or job title added during official functions.
  • Martial Pacifist: Greatly prefers and strives towards peaceful solutions.
  • The McCoy: Despite being mild-mannered, he appeals strongly to emotion and humanistic traits, if a touch bit melancholic.
  • Metaphorgotten: When explaining alien concepts to others, Remara often tries comparing it to something more familiar...before promptly realising quite vocally said comparison is inaccurate. Remara: [Explaining how the Iconian space gates work] Now, let's imagine the space-time continuum as this napkin, and the glass tumbler as the tachyoharmonic subspace distortion upon which the napkin is draped over so that you have bits hanging equally on both sides. [beat] Actually, let's not.
  • Omnidisciplinary Scientist: Occasionally subverted when he has to call on a junior officer within his department with the specialist knowledge appropriate to the situation at hand to back him up.
  • Ship Tease: With Kellat - he gets invited to dinner with her family in one episode, although it turned out Kellat's parents were simply glad to have the company of another Cardassian in Starfleet. The matter is never bought up again afterwards.
  • Stepford Smiler: Barber is understandably creeped out by this.
  • Uncoffee: Cardassian Fish Juice, drank hot during breakfast and repugnant to most humanoids. He refuses to start work or talk to anyone until he has finished his daily serving.
  • The Unfavourite: Due to pursuing the sciences rather than joining the Cardassian Defense Force like his older brother.

Lieutenant Commander V'rin K'vov

Voiced by: TBD

The Odyssey's Chief Engineer, a soft-spoken and very much reserved giant of a male Andorian, who more closely fits the norm of his species. His primary responsibility is to keep the Odyssey's Faster-Than-Light Travel Warp Core working and leading maintenance / damage control teams, as well as being the can-do counterpart to Science Officer Remara's know-how when it comes to Imported Alien Phlebotinum.

  • The Comically Serious: Considering his Andorian upbringing and antics the rest of the cast inadvertently get involved in, you have to wonder why the guy hasn't lost his mind yet.
    • During one Groundhog Day Loop episode, each of K'vov's iterations starts with him walking down a corridor and constantly getting being bumped by Ensign Akorem stumbling out a set of doors. One of the later iterations has K'vov deciding to shove Akorem back through said door.
  • Commonality Connection: Gradually becomes friends with Ong and Remara for their mutual interest in music.
    • Interestingly, they hold said interest for different reasons - Ong enjoys making music for what it is, while Remara is merely fascinated with the scientific principles involved. K'vov's just content that he has a chance to polish up on the flabbjellah that all Andorians are supposed to be proficient at as a matter of principle.
  • Genius Bruiser: Equally adept at repairing combadges and unfamiliar alien devices as he is with lifting and carrying heavy equipment.
  • Gentle Giant
  • The Engineer
  • Married to the Job: K'vov's dedication to his responsibilities as Chief Engineer leaves him with practically no time to explore any non-professional relationship.
  • Mr. Fixit: The go-to guy for the Odyssey's Phlebotinum Breakdown.
  • Not Distracted by the Sexy: Trapped with Shran aboard a damaged shuttlecraft in a rapidly-decaying orbit around a star, K'vov immediately gets to work repairing subsystems. Towards the end, the internal temperature becomes unbearable for Shran, who starts taking off layers of her uniform. K'vov's response: K'vov: Environmental control should be back online soon - you might need this. [Offers own uniform jacket to Shran]
  • Oblivious to Love: Completely unaware of Shran's amorous hints. Shran: You know, maybe after you finish your shift-
    K'vov: [To engineering crewman] -Make sure you realign those relays properly this time! [beat] Sorry, Miss Shran, you were saying?
    Shran: ...would you like to have some Raktajino [coffee] sometime?
    K'vov: With two ice cubes, please. Leave it on my desk.
    [K'vov leaves while consulting his PADD. Shran sighs, dejected.]
  • Rubber-Forehead Alien: Those two knobbed head antennae all Andorians have.
  • Techno Wizard
  • The Stoic
  • Verbal Tic: Does not use contractions.
  • Walking Techfix: Especially with non-Federation alien tech. Downplayed in that it still takes him a reasonably realistic amount of time, though.
  • Workaholic: Remained behind on the Odyssey to run subsystem diagnostics in "Death in Paradise", whilst everyone else took shore leave. Ultimately coaxed into beaming down to the resort by being asked to fix a restaurant's "faulty" food replicator.

Doctor (Lieutenant Commander) Lelania Tigan

Voiced by: TBD

The Odyssey's Chief Medical Officer, a kind-hearted unjoined (i.e. lacking the Symbiont slug thing in her abdomen) female Trill with an impeccable bedside manner. Although experienced as a physician, she has spent the majority of her career working at Starfleet Medical on Earth, and thus is quite unfamiliar to the fast-paced and harsher realities of serving aboard a starship on long-term exploration missions. Nevertheless, over the course of the series she retains her idealism and innocence more than any other characters in the cast.

  • Break the Cutie: While held hostage in "First Do No Harm" by militants who reject Federation medical aid in favour of folk remedies, their increasingly-deranged leader resorts to psychological, and ultimately physical, torture in an attempt to convert her to their way of thinking, eventually forcing her to kill one of her captors with a laser scalpel. Although she has for the most part recovered by the following episode and returned to her usual self, the harrowing experience certainly has had some effect on her.
  • Combat Medic: Subverted. Despite having sparring sessions with Barber after "First Do No Harm", Tigan remains quite reluctant to inflict harm on others, unless in self-defense and only when absolutely necessary.
  • Continuity Nod: Her surname suggests she is a distant relative of Ezri Tigan, the second host of the Dax symbiont from Deep Space Nine.
  • Ms. Fanservice: The cute type, especially in the obligatory Beach Episode "Death in Paradise".
  • Genki Girl: One of the more bubbly characters in the main cast.
  • Green-Skinned Space Babe: Partially subverted in that her skin tone and hair color are more or less "normal" by human standards. But boy, do those spots go all the way down.
  • Hospital Hottie
  • I Owe You My Life: Barber's close friendship with Tigan is initially based on this; then-Ensign Barber was gravely injured during her escape with Ensign Zhang from the doomed Bellerophon in the pilot episode teaser, and was bought aboard a Starfleet medical ship. Then-Nurse Tigan was perceptive enough to discover from Barber's medical records that she was dangerously allergic to the commonly-used Metorapan anaesthetic, and intervened just before a clueless fellow Nurse could administer the (potentially fatal) hypospray injection.
  • Jumped At The Call: Barber had persuaded Zhang to sign her on as the ship's doctor as a personal favour; Tigan was all too eager to accept.
